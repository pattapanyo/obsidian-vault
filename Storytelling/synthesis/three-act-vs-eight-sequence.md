# Three-Act vs Eight-Sequence Structure

## The Short Answer
They are not competing systems — they are different zoom levels of the same story.

## Comparison

| | Three-Act | Eight-Sequence |
|--|-----------|---------------|
| **What it tells you** | The shape of the whole story | What to write next |
| **Granularity** | Broad (3 containers) | Fine (8 mini-movies) |
| **Strongest use** | Diagnosing structural problems | Writing and breaking story |
| **Weakness** | Gives no guidance inside Act 2 | Can feel mechanical if applied rigidly |
| **Origin** | Aristotle → Hollywood | [[entities/Frank Daniel]] at USC |

## How They Fit Together

Act 1          Act 2                    Act 3
|——————|——————————————————————————|——————|
S1  S2    S3      S4    S5   S6    S7  S8

Sequences 1-2 map to Act 1. Sequences 3-6 fill Act 2. Sequences 7-8 cover Act 3.

## Practical Advice
Most working screenwriters think in both simultaneously. Use three-act to check your story's overall health. Use eight-sequence to figure out what scene to write today.
