# Addiction Loop

## Definition
A four-step engagement pattern that repeatedly activates audience neurochemistry to keep attention: Stakes → Big Question → Head Fake → Rehook.

## The Four Steps
- Stakes: Establish what could be lost (context/setting that matters).
- Big Question: Pose a clear, urgent Yes/No question the audience wants answered.
- Head Fake: Offer a plausible but misleading payoff that delays resolution and triggers dopamine.
- Rehook: Introduce a new complication or revelation that renews the cycle.

Repeat the loop across beats and scenes to sustain attention without sacrificing story logic.

## Application
Use the loop to structure scenes and sequences (not to deceive the audience permanently). The head fake should feel earned; the rehook should escalate stakes or reveal character. Works well with the [[concepts/major-dramatic-question]] and scene-level beats.
